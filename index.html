Explore the Power of Science

Learn how FORCE, MOTION, and ENERGY work together to make everything move in our world.

By : Sabina Santillan

FORCE

Force is a push or pull that can change the movement of an object.

Important Things About Force

  • Force can make objects move.
  • Force can stop moving objects.
  • Measured in Newtons (N).
  • Gravity is an example of force.

How Does It Work?

When force is applied to an object, it changes the object's speed, direction, or shape. Bigger force means bigger movement.

MOTION

Motion happens when an object changes position over time.

Important Things About Motion

  • Motion depends on speed and direction.
  • Objects move because of force.
  • Measured using distance and time.
  • Can be fast, slow, straight, or circular.

How Does It Work?

Motion occurs when force acts on an object. Cars, bicycles, and even planets move because forces affect them.

ENERGY

Energy is the ability to do work or cause movement.

Important Things About Energy

  • Energy can change forms.
  • Examples are heat, light, and electricity.
  • Energy is needed for motion.
  • Cannot be created or destroyed.

How Does It Work?

Energy transfers from one object to another. A moving object has kinetic energy, while stored energy is called potential energy.
